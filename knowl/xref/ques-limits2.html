Question 2.1.9. Finding Geometric Series.
Calculuate the geometric series corresponding to \(\frac{1}{2}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^0 + \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^1 + \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^2 + \ldots \end{equation*}
Solution.
The value of \(r\) in this case is \(\frac{1}{2}\text{,}\) which is less than \(1\text{,}\) so our formula applies.
\begin{equation*} \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^0 + \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^1 + \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^2 + \ldots = \dfrac{1}{1-1/2} = \dfrac{1}{1/2} = 2. \end{equation*}
